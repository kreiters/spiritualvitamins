“For what was written in earlier times was written for our instruction, that through perseverance and the encouragement of the Scripture we might have hope.” Romans 15:4

Studying the Bible informs us about the life and purpose of Jesus and your eternal life with God in Heaven. Did you know that God created you to live forever? When your life is over here on earth, whether it is 50 years or 100 years, you will continue to exist. For those who love Christ and make Him king in their life they will dwell in heaven for endless time. This is God’s perfect plan for a new earth where sin will not exist and love will reign. John 3:15 “That whoever believes in Him (Jesus) should not perish but have eternal life.”

For those who do not accept Christ’s free gift before they die, their eternity will be in the Lake of Fire. There they will experience endless pain and suffering. Revelation 20:15 “Anyone not found written in the Book of Life was cast into the Lake of Fire.”

Facts about the Scripture are: The Bible has 66 books within its cover. It was written over a period of 1,500 years by more than 40 authors who were inspired by the Holy Spirit. God’s message concerns the need to reconcile people’s relationship with a sinless loving God. Galatians 3:26; “For you all are sons of God through Christ Jesus.” Galatians 3:14 “That the blessings of Abraham might come upon Gentiles in Christ Jesus, that we might receive the promise of the Spirit through faith.”

God gave mankind the Bible as a help to learn great knowledge, gain wisdom, understand history, grow in love for Him as our Creator, and to receive the forever peace that Jesus’s death bestowed upon us.

Studying Scripture is vital! Many famous Theologians have studied the Bible in many different ways. Here are some ideas. (1) Read 1 Psalm and 1 Proverb daily; (2) Plan to read through the entire Bible in 1 year; (3) Examine as many verses as you can of God’s promises and claim them; (4) Investigate one subject such as Sin or Salvation and learn what God says about it; (5) Memorize some important verses.

“Nature forms us; Sin deforms us; School informs us; and Christ transforms us.” Anonymous
