January 2024 - Angels as Spiritual Servents

"The Angel of the Lord who has redeemed me from all evil, Bless the Lord." Genesis 48:16

As we begin the new year, may I open the Celestial door and with you observe God's heavenly messengers? These messengers are the angels that praise God night and day. Angels are the topic for this month and are mentioned in Genesis guarding the Tree of Life through Revelation at the final judgment Psalm 103:20-22. Let's open the Bible and see what we can learn about God"s angels.

Angels are created beings who are masculine gender according to names given in various verses (see Jude 9 & Matthew 12:26). The Archangel, Michael, Gabriel, Satan (or Beelzebub) and Cherubim are a fragment of the Host of angels God created. Angels dwell in heaven and are summoned by God to be messengers and servants. They are capable of traveling between God's throne and earth. They are not babies nor women from what we read in the Bible. We read descriptions of angels in Revelation 4:4 and Ezekiel 1:5-14.

I used three resources. Primary was the Bible. In addition, The Bible Knowledge Commentary written by James F. Walvood and Roy B. Zuck. Both men were Professors at Dallas Seminary. My third source was Nave's Topical Bible written by Orville J. Nave L.L.D.,L.D. Pastor Nave spent14 years of untiring study before writing his Topical Bible. Here are eight additional verses about angels.

1. THE TRINITY AND ANGEL OF GOD: Read Genesis 2:1 and Revelation 22:8-9. We are warned not to worship angels. They are servants of God.

2. A CELESTIAL SPIRIT: Luke 2:13-14. Revelation 22:6.

3. ANGEL POWER: 2 Peter 2:4. Revelation 4:11.

4. ANGELS ANNOUNCE THREE BIRTHS: Samson, Judges 13:3, John The Baptist, Matthew 1:20, and JESUS, Luke 1:30.

5. TO WORSHIP GOD: Philippians 2:9-11, Revelation 19:4-6

6. ARE WISE, HOLY, MIGHTY, INNUMERABLE: Deuteronomy 32:36

7. SECOND COMING OF CHRIST: Luke 16:22, Revelation 4:8.

8. ANGELS AT JUDGMENT: Matthew 25:31, Mark 8:38, Jude 14 & Jude 15.

As we exit the gates of heaven together we can still hear the Angels saying, Holy, Holy, Holy, LORD GOD ALMIGHTY. How shall we respond? 1 Peter 1:13-16. Amen
