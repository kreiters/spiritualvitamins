Philippians 4:8-9 “In conclusion, my brothers, fill your minds with those things that are good and deserve praise: things that are true, noble, right, pure, lovely, and honorable. Put into practice what you learned and received from me, both from my words and from my deeds.” (Good News for Modern Man)

As the days and weeks have collected in 2021 the news reports and actions of leaders all over our world have been worrying, lawless, and in shambles. I have found it difficult to stay happy and optimistic about our nation’s future. In reading Philippians 4:8-9 however, I understand that Paul, the author, meant these passages for growth, good instruction and a positive action for both his times and ours. I encourage you to join me as we dig into these 8 scripture skills and seek a deeper personal understanding of God’s truth.

TRUTH = The word translates to be SURE. Only God’s Word, the Bible, can be known and depended upon for absolute TRUTH.

HONEST = Here is another basic word which means GOOD or TRUE. Many times the reports we receive in this present age are slanted to tell us information that is other than completely honest.

RIGHT = The full biblical meaning of RIGHT is JUST or JUSTICE. As flawed people we fail perfect justice. Only GOD’S justice is perfect!

PURE = GENUINE or UNDEFILED are the hallmark of this word. Back in the 1940’s the product Ivory Soap had a slogan which called their soap 99 and 44/100% pure. Today we are called to follow Christ’s example of living a life of PURE moral behavior.

LOVELY = We seek this word’s meaning, which is BEAUTIFUL as our outward character. Our LORD seeks a person who speaks lovely words, thinks lovely thoughts, and uses lovely actions.

GOOD REPUTE = Here is a real challenge. REPUTE translates as REPORT. GOD has sent us many good reports. The major report is the Good News of Jesus Christ. EXCELLENCE = This skill is translated PERFECTION or PREEMINENT. This would describe one such as a king, ruler, or lord. This describes GOD, the LORD of LIFE.

PRAISE= Translated from the Greek we have the meaning of BLESSED or COMMENDED. We need to continually remember all the blessings we receive come continually from our Heavenly Father
