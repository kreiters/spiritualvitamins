February 2024 - Love

Deuteronomy 6:5 "You shall love the LORD your GOD with all your heart, and with all your soul, and with all your might."

The month of February is often used to put love in the spotlight. It is Heart Health month, Baby Cupid with a bow and arrow is used to promote romantic love, and Cherrios uses heart shaped little O's to sell their product. There is nothing wrong for the world to use these symbols to target love, but it misses Cupid's target and is very shallow compared to the richness of GOD's love.

The first mention of Valentine centers around two different Catholic Popes who attempted to promote love and marriage under the name St. Valentine in the Third century. They were beheaded on February 14, 496 by the Roman Emperor Claudius II. Love suffered a set back.

God's love is Agape love. It is used over 500 times in Scripture and has a much deeper meaning than romantic love or superficial head knowledge.

What then is love? Beginning in 1 Corinthians 13 verse 4 we find Paul's Biblical definition of love. "Love is patient, love is kind, and is not jealous; love does not brag and is not arrogant, does not act unbecomingly; it does not seek its own, is not provoked, does not take into account a wrong suffered, does not rejoice in unrighteousness, but rejoices with truth; bears all things, believes all things, hopes all things, endures all things. Love never fails."

God's love is shared by Christians and is the product of a regenerate nature. Christians know God, not just with head knowledge but with deeper understanding that God is love, which He shares with us as we confess Jesus as Lord. We understand Jesus Christ's birth, death and resurrection as being the love gift to God's created beings as payment for our sin.

What then is the evidence of an unloving person? An unloving person is revealed by the fact that the person does not love God or His Son. This is a rejection of God's free gift of His Son Jesus and bears the penalty of death.

Who does scripture command to love? Christians are commanded to love their neighbors as we love ourselves; husbands love your wives; pastors love their congregations, and we must even love our enemies. In 1 John 4:21 we read "And this commandment we have from Him, that the one who loves God should love his brother also."

What does the disciple Peter say in 1 Peter 4:8 that sums up the entire love responsibility? "Above all, keep fervent in your love for one another, because love covers a multitude of sins." This is such an important command that Paul says in 1 Corinthians 16:22 "If any one does not love the Lord, let him be accursed." Accursed is a very strong word meaning being separated from Christ and missing the forever life in heaven.

May you find greater love in the month of February.
