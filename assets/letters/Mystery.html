March 2024 - March Mysteries

Matthew 13:10-11 "And the Disciples came and said to Him (Jesus), why do you speak to them in parables? Because it has been given to you to know the mysteries of the kingdom of heaven, but to them it has not been given."

I love a good mystery - a book by Agatha Christy or an evening with sherlock Holmes. I enjoy watching the detective untangle clues and decipher the complex plots.

Solomon and Jesus are recorded to be the wisest men to have lived. Solomon used proverbs which are word pictures to present a truth. Jesus used parables which is a story constructed to tell a previous unknown truth. The Parables are not understood by those who have eyes but cannot see and ears who cannot hear. Jesus used parables to teach about future events that were not given in the Old Testament about the kingdom of God. They were hidden from Israel and the Pharisees. The disciples and true believers can now fully understand these kingdom truths.

PARABLE OF THE SOWER: Matthew 13:18-23 The good news of the gospel will be rejected by most people.

PARABLE OF THE PEARL OF GREAT PRICE: Matthew 13:45-46 Christ gave His life to provide redemption for the church.

PARABLE OF THE MUSTARD SEED: Matthew 13:31-32 Believers and unbelievers will grow rapidly from a small beginning.

PARABLE OF THE HIDDEN TREASURE: Matthew 13:44 Christ came to purchase (redeem) Israel.

PARABLE OF THE DRAGNET: Matthew 13:47-50 Angels will come and separate the wicked from the righteous when Christ comes.

PARABLE OF THE WHEAT AND THE TARES(WEEDS): Matthew 13:24-30 People of faith and people of false faith will exist together between Christ's two advents.

PARABLE OF THE 10 MINAS: Luke 19:11-14 Jesus teaches the disciples and the nation about stewardship and responsibilities.

PARABLES OF CHRIST AND CHURCH: Ephesians 5:28 Christ is the Savior of the church. The great mystery is the husband and wife becoming one and the bond of Jesus and the church.

May God bless you as you discover the truths contained in these mysteries!
