Thanksgiving Letter 2023

Psalm 26:7 "I will wash my hands in innocence; so I will go about your altar, O Lord. that I may proclaim with a voice of thanksgiving, and tell of all Your wondrous works."

I recently read a quote which consists of 5 words that describe the essence of thanksgiving. "God's giving deserves our thanksgiving!"

To develop this biblical attitude of gratitude takes purpose and concentration for most of us. We are not born with gratitude. We are born selfish! "Feed me, I'm hungry." "Change me, I'm wet" "That's my toy". "Do it my way." "I need a new outfit." It takes a desire to be grateful continually for all of the blessings God lavishes on us. You may say, "So far what you've written is not new to me"

Why do I need to change? If I could change what do I need to do to accomplish an attitude of gratitude? These are the questions on my mind. To begin, I believe change does not happen over night for me. I believe gratitude happens like the drip, drip, drip of a melting icicle! It is the slow and gradual accomplishment of reaching the much desired goal.

1. I believe all of us have room to change. God has blessed each of us so bountifully. The writer of Psalm 57:7 expresses it as God's steadfast love for us. We should have a steadfast goal to seek gratitude. "My heart is steadfast, O God; my heart is steadfast, I will sing praises."

2. We are always urged to pray for needs. We may ask the Holy Spirit to make us attuned to our selfishness, our unthinking attitudes and adverse thoughts that keep us from reaching our thankful goal.

3. I hope that as you read the Spiritual Vitamins the month you will agree that the result of working diligently toward a thankful heart and expressing it continually develops a happy heart!

Psalm 100:4 "Enter His gates with Thanksgiving, and into His courts with Praise."
