Seven Steps to Follow Faith in Christ

The very best definition of faith is found in Hebrews 11:1 in the Bible. "Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen.‚Äù This verse teaches we can be sure about God, (hypostasis). Being certain (elencho), about unseen hopes and realities. The Bible says Faith wins Gods acceptance and reward.

The next step after Faith is Virtue. The disciple, Paul is concerned about the immorality in the Corinthians Church and quotes a pagan writer, Menander, with an ancient definition. "Bad company corrupts good character. My grandmother used to quote, "One rotten apple spoils the whole barrel."

Knowledge is the third topic after Faith and Virtue. Knowledge is understanding and related to discern. Love of Christ surpasses knowledge of Christ.

Self-Control is fourth in our walk. It is a big challenge for most of us. We see the lack of it daily on television. The Bible lists 19 negative traits of faithlessness in 2 Timothy 3:1-9. without self- control. Galatians 5:23 names it as fruit from the Holy Spirit.

The fifth step is Perseverance. Iwo Churches in Revelation were commended for their steadfast perseverance. Thyatira is promised authority over the nations, and the Philidelphia church would be a pillar in the temple of God. Rewards are given by God for perseverance.

Godliness, which is next step holds a great mystery. The mystery concerns Christ and was hidden in previous years but now revealed. Here is the revelation. 1. Christ appeared in body. 2. Was vindicated by the Holy Spirit. 3. Seen by Angels. 4. Preached among nations. 5. Believed on by the world. 6. Taken up in glory.

Our last step, number seven is Brotherly kindness and Brotherly love. We who are a part of the Christian faith are expected to experience God's Faithful Love and reproduce it in our life.

We end with a snapshot of the facets of faith as we remember the scripture on Love. 1 Corinthians 13:13. The images of Faith, Hope and Love are excellent, but Love is eternal.
