“We glide along the tides of time as swiftly as a dashing river and then vanish as quickly as a dream.” Psalm 90:5 (Living Bible)

As I was deciding how to begin this new year, many topics floated through my mind. This last year found all of us frustrated in many ways, which seemed to change the use of our time.

What is time? It is what God made for us to use while we lives on this earth. We all have a birth - a beginning of our years, days, hours, minutes, and seconds - and a death, which is the end of our time on this earth. It is a proven fact that 100% of all people who are born die.

Did you know that time continues for all of us when God calls us to our eternal home? There we no longer count the years, days, hours, and minutes of our lives. We are all created by God for eternity. Try to imagine eternity. It is forever!

How we spend our time in our present lives and the choices we make will determine how and where we will spend the rest of our eternal existence. The most important decision you will ever make is what you will do with Jesus. Will you accept His free gift of eternal life in heaven, wrapped in love, joy, and peace, or will you make excuses to reject the free gift provided by Jesus’ death on the cross? His death was payment for your sin. To reject that free gift will land you in hell for all eternity!

I made the choice to place my faith in Jesus fifty years ago and I congratulate you if have also placed your faith in Jesus. If you haven’t decided to place your faith and eternal destiny in Christ’s hands, I urge you to do so today.

The apostle Paul wrote to this to the people living in Corinth: “Behold now is the acceptable time, behold now is the day of salvation” (2 Corinthians 6:3) What he was saying is, “After you die, it will be too late!”
