“Remember the angels who did not stay within the limits of their proper authority, but abandoned their own dwelling place: God has kept them in the darkness below, bound in eternal chains for that great Day on which they will be condemned.” (Jude 6)

My birthday falls on Halloween, and each year I notice that on October 1st, scary movies, television shows, and ghastly yard decor appear. Horror shows and evil depicted in these ways do not entertain me. So I decided to speak out against evil this month.

*Why is evil so popular and fascinating to people? I submit that it is the work of the Fallen Angel, Satan, and his angel followers who tried to take God’s power while in heaven (Luke 10:18). *Evil is real and according to scripture evil has many names. We read about him in Genesis when he tempted Adam and Eve. Here Satan is called Serpent. Other names are the Devil, the Dragon of end times, (Revelation 12:9) and Beelzebul (Mark 3:22 and Luke 11:15,18 and 19). *Satan is a fallen Angel who wanted to be God with God’s powers (Luke 10: 18). *The work of the Devil is to seek out and attempt to deceive people into turning away from God and turn hearts to worship him. *Other facts about Satan are that God has temporarily allowed Satan to rule this present world (John 12:31 & John 14:30). Satan has power but it is limited (1 John 5:19). 1 John 4:4 tells how Christians are protected. Jesus, himself, was tempted after being baptized and Jesus used Scripture to defeat the devil (Mark 1:13). Satan entered into Judas Iscariot to betray Jesus and Judas did not protect himself (Luke 23:2-6). *We can learn important lessons from both Jesus and Judas. *Satan’s final destiny is described in Revelation 20:10, where we read “Then the Devil, who deceived them, was thrown into the lake of fire and sulphur, where the beast and the false prophet had already been thrown; and they will be tormented day and night, forever and ever.”

*In closing, I quote Acts 26:18 where Jesus tells Paul: “You are to open their eyes and turn them from the darkness to the light, and from the power of Satan to God, so that through faith in ME they will have their sins forgiven and receive their place among God’s people.”
