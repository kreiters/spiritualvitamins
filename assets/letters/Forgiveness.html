“Forgiveness is to be set loose from sin.”

The above is a quote by G. Campbell Morgan and states a perfect definition of the process of making our thoughts, actions or resentments pardoned with God and our fellowman. Absolution from our sin toward God or others is very necessary for our fellowship and well being. The channel for this process is forgiveness. Matthew 7:12 “Therefore whatever you want others to do for you, do so for them, for this is the Law and the Prophets.”

To understand forgiveness we must believe Romans 5:12 “Sin came into the world through one man (Adam), and his sin brought death with it.” Romans 5:8 continues the process “But God has shown us how much He loves us: while we were still sinners Christ died or us! His shed blood on the cross provided for the forgiveness that we enjoy.

This plan provides that God is first of all, the unconditional forgiver of the sin we allow to enter our lives. However, the Lord generously forgives our sin when we sincerely ask Him to pardon us. There is no limit except one to God’s forgiveness and He promises to forget once we have been forgiven. The exception is Matthew 12:31 “For this reason I tell you: men can be forgiven any sin and any evil thing they say; but whoever says evil things against the Holy Spirit will not be forgiven.”

We must also understand that we are required to forgive our fellow men when they sin against us. This concept is much more difficult for us. We desire forgiveness from God and indeed feel grateful for it, but we feel that justice demands punishment without forgiveness for those who hurt or abuse us.

Jesus spoke to this question in Luke 17:3,4 “If your brother sins, rebuke him, and if he repents, forgive him. If he sins against you seven times in one day, and each time he comes to you saying, ‘I repent’, you must forgive him.” We must forgive as many times as forgiving is needed without conditions. If we fail to forgive we are disobeying God’s command. Then we become the sinner. Our sin then causes a break in our fellowship with God and separates us from God’s forgiveness.

Where is our THANKFULNESS for this wonderful gift of Forgiveness? Psalm 92:1,5 “It is good to give Thanks to the LORD. How great are your works, O LORD. Your thoughts are very deep.”
