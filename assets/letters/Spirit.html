Psalm 104:30 “You, Lord, send forth Your Spirit, they (people) are created; And You renew the face of the earth.”

Since March blows in on the wind, Tim suggested I write about the third person of the Trinity, the Holy Spirit. He is described as the wind (we feel the wind but do not see it), as fire (which is helpful but can consume and obliterate if necessary), and water (which is very vital and refreshing to humans).

All three persons were present at the creation of the world. Genesis 1:1 “In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth.”The Bible says in Genesis 1:26, “God said let us make man in our image, according to our likeness.” This word “us” is very important. In the Hebrew language the word for us is plural. It means more than one. It reveals that all three members of the trinity were active and responsible for the creation of the world and mankind.

At Jesus’ baptism in Mark 1:10 “And immediately coming up from the water he (John) saw the heavens parting and the Spirit descending upon Jesus like a dove. Then a voice came from heaven,’You are my beloved Son in whom I am well pleased.’ Here is the Trinity together. Jesus a human, the dove as the Holy Spirit, and the voice of God coming from heaven. The 3rd time the Trinity is mentioned is when Gabriel appears to Mary and announced Christ’s birth Luke 1:30-35.

Here are some of the attributes of the Holy Spirit: His WORK: In John 3:5, Jesus said, ”Most assuredly, I say to you, unless one is born of water and the Spirit, he cannot enter the kingdom of God.” His TEACHING: Luke 12:12 says “For the Holy Spirit will teach you in that very hour what you should say.” From 1Corinthians 2:13, “These things we also speak not in man’s wisdom but that which the Holy Spirit teaches .... comparing spiritual things with spiritual.” His HELP: Romans 8:26 says, “Likewise the Spirit also helps in our weaknesses .... but the Spirit makes intercession (with God) for us.” His GIFT OF FRUIT: In Galatians 5:23 and 24, Paul lists 9 character traits the Holy Spirit produces in a believer’s life. “But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, and self-control.” His Warnings: Do Not Quench the Holy Spirit (1 Thessalonians 5: 19); Do Not Grieve the Holy Spirit (Ephesians 4:30); Do not commit Blasphemy against the Holy Spirit, because it is the only sin in Scripture that is unforgivable.

My letter comes with LOVE. I pray that you will take God’s Word’s to heart, will accept God’s Salvation, and will never blaspheme the Holy Spirit.
